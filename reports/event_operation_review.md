# Event Operation Review

## Preparation Work

Before the charity sale, the main data work was to turn donated items and
support records into organized spreadsheets. This made preparation easier
because each team could see what had been counted, categorized, estimated, and
assigned.

## Item Counting and Classification

Inventory records helped group donated items by category, quantity, condition,
team, and booth area. This reduced confusion during preparation and made it
easier to decide where different item groups should go.

## Value Estimation

Estimated item values helped with early planning. They were not exact sale
prices, but they gave a useful reference for setting simple, fair prices before
the event.

## Online Spreadsheet Tracking

Online spreadsheets were used to track donations, item groups, preparation
progress, and team responsibilities. Having shared records helped reduce
missing information and made post-event review easier.

## Item Allocation

Item allocation depended on category, size, condition, and expected buyer
interest. Smaller fast-moving items were easier to place on shared tables,
while larger or more fragile items needed clearer booth space.

## Booth Layout Planning

Booth layout records helped compare estimated item counts with actual item
counts. Larger categories and smaller fast-moving items needed more visible
space, especially on the shared table.

## Team Coordination

Team-level summaries combined donation, inventory, and sale records. This
helped review each team's work without relying only on memory after the event.

## Post-Event Review

After the sale, the cleaned data made it possible to review category revenue,
booth revenue, item quantities, and estimate vs actual price differences.

## What Could Be Improved Next Time

- Add clearer item ID labels before the sale.
- Track sale time to understand busy periods.
- Record booth visitor flow if possible.
- Use a more consistent data collection form.
- Review unsold items more carefully after the event.
